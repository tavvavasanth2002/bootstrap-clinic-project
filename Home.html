DIAGNOSTICS

Laboratory

A medical laboratory performs diagnostic tests on blood, urine, and tissue samples. It supports disease detection, treatment planning, and health monitoring using specialized equipment and trained professionals in clinical analysis.

X-Ray

An X-ray is a medical imaging technique that uses electromagnetic radiation to view internal body structures, especially bones. It helps diagnose fractures, infections, and other conditions quickly and noninvasively



ECG

An ECG (electrocardiogram) records the heart’s electrical activity to detect rhythm abnormalities, heart attacks, and other conditions. It’s a quick, non-invasive test using electrodes placed on the chest and limbs

TMT

A TMT (Treadmill Test) evaluates heart function under physical stress. Patients walk on a treadmill while ECG monitors detect blood flow issues, arrhythmias, or coronary artery disease symptoms during exertion



Genetic Screening Test

Genetic screening tests analyze DNA to detect inherited mutations linked to diseases. They help assess risk, guide treatment, and inform family planning through methods like sequencing or microarray analysis

Blood Test

A blood test analyzes components like cells, proteins, and chemicals to assess health. It helps diagnose conditions, monitor treatments, and detect infections, organ issues, or nutritional deficiencies through lab evaluation.
